Category Details
Regular Tea Regular tea, also known as black tea, is a type of tea that is more oxidized than green, oolong, and white teas. It is made from the leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant. Regular tea is the most common type of tea consumed in the Western world
Masala Tea Masala tea, also known as chai, is a flavored tea beverage that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It is made by brewing black tea with a mixture of aromatic spices, milk, and sweeteners. Masala tea is known for its rich and flavorful taste, with a combination of warm spices.
Milk Tea Milk tea is a beverage made by combining tea with milk or a milk substitute. It is popular in many parts of the world, including Asia, Europe, and North America. Milk tea can be made with various types of tea and can be customized with different flavors and sweeteners./td>
Green Tea Green tea is a type of tea that is made from the leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant, just like black tea and oolong tea. However, what sets green tea apart is that it undergoes minimal oxidation during processing, allowing it to retain its natural green color and many beneficial compounds.
